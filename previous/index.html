  • 2014

    Postmortem image processing pipeline I needed a way to visualize a gross neuropathology in 3D. To do so, I built a cloud-based imaging pipeline that took in situ hybridized image sections of neocortex, registered them to each other, and generated a density cloud approximation for each laminar structure. This pipeline lead to a detailed description of patches of abberant expression in the neocortex of individuals with autism.

  • 2013

    Rendering connectivity Using the Allen Institute mouse connectivity data set, I rendered a series of generative projection atlases with each projections colored by origin.

  • 2013

    Multi-headset free-moving gaze tracking system I combined the pupil gaze-tracking headset with AR-based landmarks to power gaze-bazed games in a Unity environment.

    Qualcomm Uplinq hackathon Using the Snapdragon Gaze SDK, I built a prototype for early Autism screening. Captured the Grand Prize at Qualcomm's Uplinq 2012 hackathon.

    USB Ultrasound for Android I reverse-engineered a veterinary USB ultrasound and built the first portable ultrasound app on an android device.

  • 2012

    FigureZero was an interactive zoomable canvas on for poster authors to annotate their PDF poster. It was tested at the 2012 Society for Neuroscience meeting as a way to present and share digital posters.

  • 2012

    OpenWorm Browser for iOS Working with the Openworm.org team, I ported the open-3d-viewer (aka the Google Body Browser) to iOS & GLKit. The app allowed a user to explore the anatomy of c. elegans. It is open-source and available on the app store.

  • 2011

    Cognitive Consilience Cognitive Consilience was a web and app-based visualization tool for neural circuits present in the human neocortex. It used multiple layers to display different circuits, along with their supporting references in an interactive interface.

  • 2011

    WholeSlide The WholeSlide app was developed to view and interact with large gigapixel+ wholeslide images hosted online. The app allowed users to zoom into many different image types: from neuroanatomy and developmental biology to pathology and histology education resources.

  • 2008

    Cerebrii - 3D brain atlas for iPhone First 3D brain atlas for iPhone. Interactive visualization of the SPL brain atlas with medical study guide and quiz. Extended to display fiber bundles from DTI. Featured in iPhone development book(s).

  • 2007

    DC Metro brain map An anatomically-correct project of neuroanatomy onto the DC brain map. Created as a t-shirt design for the Pitt + CMU Center for the Neural Basis of Cognition. I'm still amazed how well some of the structures and circuits mapped to the map.
